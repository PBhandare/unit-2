Understanding Sheybogan County Area Climate Normals

This interactive map visualizes 30-year climate normals around the Sheyboygan County, WI area, summarizing average temperature, maximum and minimum temperature, precipitation, and snowfall for each month. The proportional symbols show the magnitude of each variable at individual ZIP codes, with color coding to distinguish precipitation (blue), snowfall (white), and temperature (orange). The data come from the Wisconsin State Climatology Office, which aggregates historical observations from the National Climatic Data Center, and ZTCA datasets. Use the month slider and variable selector to explore how these climate patterns vary across the state and throughout the year. While there are over 30 counties represnted on this map, they really fall under the umbrella of 18 weather stations. A quick look through the variables on the map over the year show obvious spatial homogeneity. In a sense, this brings the question the utility of climate normals at such granularity. Its not immediately clear if lake effects (like cooler summers or warmer winters) exist on the variables. Do grassy features trap more water? Perhaps, it would be more pertinent to look at a wider regional extent to capture more climatic differences.
